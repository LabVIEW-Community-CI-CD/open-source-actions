# MEETING-POLICY.md

> 🕒 This document defines the meeting policies of the NI Open-Source Program, including frequency, structure, scoping, and participant expectations.  
> Its purpose is to protect contributor time, prevent misalignment, and ensure program-wide consistency in how decisions are surfaced and tracked.

---

## §1. Purpose of This Policy

Meetings exist to:
- Facilitate strategic governance decisions (program-wide)
- Support IP-specific collaboration when GitHub async is insufficient
- Provide structure, not bureaucracy

This document applies to **all meetings coordinated by the Program Manager or Program-affiliated organizers** under the open-source initiative.

---

## §2. Meeting Types

| Meeting Type | Description | Typical Participants |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------------|
| **Governance Meeting** | Focuses on scoring, program policies, contributor recognition, or strategic evaluation topics | Program Manager, SteerCo, selected NI leads |
| **IP-Specific Meeting** | Scoped to a single repository or tightly related IP set | SteerCo members, maintainers, contributors |
| **Core Team Sync (optional)** | Operational check-in between PM and selected NI stakeholders | Internal use (can be deprecated over time) |

---

## §3. Cadence Policy

- Meetings **alternate weekly** between governance and IP-specific sessions.
- Recurring meetings are scheduled but **can be canceled or replaced** with async threads when appropriate.
- SteerCo members are not expected to attend both weekly; only the relevant type to their role.

---

## §4. Agenda & Scope Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| 📅 **Pre-published Agenda** | Must be shared at least 24 hours before the meeting |
| 🔒 **Scoped Topics** | Agendas should limit discussion to 1–3 focused topics |
| 📎 **Documented Outcomes** | Key takeaways must be recorded in GitHub Discussions, Issues, or governance commits |
| ❗ **No Ambush Policy** | No new policy or scoring decisions may be made without prior agenda notice |

---

## §5. Participation Expectations

- Attendance is voluntary but expected when agenda overlaps with an individual’s area of leadership
- Anyone who joins is expected to:
  - Arrive prepared
  - Stay on topic
  - Ask clarifying questions early

---

## §6. Meeting Tooling & Access

| Tool | Usage |
|------|--------|
| GitHub Discussions | Canonical home for post-meeting follow-up and public traceability |
| Zoom or Teams | Primary synchronous tools, based on calendar invite |
| GitHub Projects | May be used to track action items linked to milestones |
